# You Already Know How to Build a Skill
# 你已經會建 Skill 了

> *The biggest barrier to AI isn't technology. It's the belief that you need to be technical.*
> *AI 最大的障礙不是技術。而是你以為自己需要懂技術。*

---

## The Problem No One Talks About ── 沒人討論的問題

There's a strange paradox in the AI world right now.

現在的 AI 世界有一個奇怪的悖論。

On one side, we have people trained in humanities, social sciences, and business — project managers, marketers, accountants, administrators, educators, legal professionals. These people have **strong logic, clear processes, and excellent questioning skills**. They structure complex arguments. They manage intricate workflows. They know how to verify their own work. Every single day, they exercise exactly the skills that AI needs.

一邊是人文、社會科學和商學訓練出身的人——專案經理、行銷人員、會計、行政人員、教育工作者、法務專業人士。這些人有**很強的邏輯、清楚的流程和出色的提問能力**。他們結構化複雜的論述。他們管理精密的工作流程。他們知道如何驗證自己的工作。每一天，他們都在運用 AI 所需的能力。

On the other side, we have the AI industry telling everyone they need to learn "prompt engineering," "structured workflows," "agentic patterns," and "Skill definitions." The terminology sounds technical. The tutorials are written by engineers. The examples involve code. And so these highly capable professionals look at AI and think: **"This isn't for me."**

另一邊，AI 產業告訴所有人需要學習「prompt engineering」、「結構化工作流程」、「代理模式」和「Skill 定義」。術語聽起來很技術。教學是工程師寫的。範例涉及程式碼。所以這些高度有能力的專業人士看著 AI 想：**「這不是給我的。」**

This is a tragedy. Not because they're missing out on AI — but because **AI is missing out on them**. Their domain knowledge, their process expertise, their validation instincts — these are exactly what AI needs to become genuinely useful in the real world. Without their input, AI Skills will only be built by engineers who understand code but not the work itself.

這是一個悲劇。不是因為他們錯過了 AI——而是因為 **AI 錯過了他們**。他們的領域知識、他們的流程專長、他們的驗證直覺——這些正是 AI 要在真實世界變得真正有用所需要的。沒有他們的投入，AI Skills 只會由懂程式碼但不懂工作本身的工程師來建構。

## The Secret: You're Already Doing It ── 祕密：你已經在做了

Let's look at what a "Skill" actually requires. In the Agentic Substrate framework, every Skill has [seven components](../01-foundations/skill-anatomy.md):

讓我們看看一個「Skill」實際上需要什麼。在 Agentic Substrate 框架中，每個 Skill 都有[七大組件](../01-foundations/skill-anatomy.md)：

| Component 組件 | Sounds Technical 聽起來很技術 | What It Actually Is 其實是什麼 |
|---|---|---|
| Identity | "Scope and role definition" | Knowing what your job is 知道你的工作是什麼 |
| Trigger Conditions | "Activation criteria" | Knowing when to start 知道什麼時候開始 |
| Process Logic | "Execution workflow" | Knowing the steps 知道步驟 |
| I/O Contract | "Input/output specification" | Knowing what you need and what you produce 知道需要什麼、產出什麼 |
| Validation Rules | "Quality assurance criteria" | Knowing how to check your work 知道怎麼檢查成果 |
| Knowledge | "Domain knowledge base" | Knowing the tricks of the trade 知道行內的訣竅 |
| Composition | "Inter-skill interfaces" | Knowing what comes before and after your task 知道你的任務前後接什麼 |

Read the right column again. **You do all of this. Every day.** You just don't call it a "Skill definition." You call it "my job."

再讀一次右邊那欄。**你每天都在做這些。**你只是不叫它「Skill 定義」。你叫它「我的工作」。

## A Story: The Accountant Who Built a Skill Without Knowing It ── 故事：不知不覺建了一個 Skill 的會計

Let's follow Mei-Ling, a senior accountant at a mid-sized company.

讓我們跟著美玲，一家中型公司的資深會計。

Mei-Ling has been doing monthly expense reconciliation for eight years. She's fast, thorough, and rarely makes mistakes. When new hires join the accounting team, she trains them — walking through the process step by step, explaining the gotchas, the edge cases, the "things the manual doesn't tell you."

美玲做月度報帳核銷已經八年了。她快速、仔細，極少出錯。新人加入會計團隊時，她負責訓練——帶他們一步一步走過流程，解釋陷阱、邊界情況、「手冊不會告訴你的事」。

One day, her company starts using an AI assistant. Mei-Ling's manager asks her to "teach the AI how to help with expense processing." Mei-Ling panics. She opens ChatGPT and types: "Help me create an AI workflow for expense processing." The response is full of terms she doesn't understand: "define your schema," "set up validation pipelines," "configure the tool chain." She closes the tab. "I'm not a programmer," she thinks. "I can't do this."

有一天，公司開始使用 AI 助理。美玲的主管要她「教 AI 怎麼幫忙處理報帳」。美玲慌了。她打開 ChatGPT 輸入：「幫我建立一個報帳處理的 AI 工作流程」。回應充滿了她不懂的術語：「定義你的 schema」、「設定驗證 pipeline」、「配置工具鏈」。她關掉頁面。「我不是程式設計師，」她想。「我做不到。」

But here's the thing. **Everything the AI needs, Mei-Ling already knows:**

但事情是這樣的。**AI 需要的一切，美玲都已經知道了：**

She knows **when** the task starts (colleagues submit receipts before the 5th). That's a trigger condition.

她知道任務**什麼時候**開始（同事在 5 號前提交單據）。那是觸發條件。

She knows **what she needs** before she can begin (receipts, expense form, last month's ledger, department budget). That's an input contract.

她知道開始前**需要什麼**（單據、報帳表、上月帳本、部門預算）。那是輸入契約。

She knows **the steps** — check amounts match, verify categories, compare against budget, enter into system, flag over-budget items, file and update ledger. That's process logic.

她知道**步驟**——核對金額一致、確認類別、對照預算、登入系統、標記超預算項目、歸檔更新帳本。那是流程邏輯。

She knows **what "done" looks like** (updated ledger, filed receipts, summary email to department head). That's an output contract.

她知道**「完成」長什麼樣子**（更新的帳本、歸檔的單據、給部門主管的摘要 email）。那是輸出契約。

She knows **how to check her work** (amounts must match exactly, every receipt needs a date and vendor name, budget overages must be flagged). That's validation rules.

她知道**怎麼檢查成果**（金額必須完全一致、每張單據要有日期和廠商名稱、超預算必須標記）。那是驗證規則。

And she knows **the tricks** (Department C always miscategorizes transportation as supplies; the system crashes over 50 entries, so batch in 30s; send reminders to known late submitters on the 1st). That's domain knowledge — the kind that no manual contains and no engineer could guess.

她還知道**訣竅**（C 部門總是把交通費歸成雜支、系統超過 50 筆就當機所以分 30 筆一批、1 號就先發提醒給常遲交的人）。那是領域知識——手冊沒有、工程師也猜不到的那種。

**Mei-Ling doesn't need to learn prompt engineering. She needs someone to tell her: "That thing you just explained to me? Write it down. That's your Skill."**

**美玲不需要學 prompt engineering。她需要有人告訴她：「你剛剛跟我解釋的那些？寫下來。那就是你的 Skill。」**

## The Gap Is Vocabulary, Not Capability ── 差距在詞彙，不在能力

Let's be explicit about what's happening:

讓我們明確說明正在發生什麼：

```
What you say every day:              What the AI world calls it:
你每天說的話：                        AI 世界怎麼稱呼：

"I know what my job is"         →    Identity / Scope Definition
「我知道我的工作是什麼」               身份/範圍定義

"I start when..."              →    Trigger Conditions
「我在...的時候開始」                  觸發條件

"First I do X, then Y"         →    Process Logic / Prompt Chaining
「我先做 X，然後做 Y」                流程邏輯/提示鏈

"I need these files to begin"  →    Input Contract
「我需要這些檔案才能開始」             輸入契約

"I deliver this report"        →    Output Contract
「我交付這份報告」                     輸出契約

"I check these three things"   →    Validation Rules
「我檢查這三件事」                     驗證規則

"Let me tell you a shortcut"   →    Domain Knowledge
「讓我告訴你一個捷徑」                 領域知識

"After I'm done, someone       →    Composition / Skill Chaining
 else does..."                       組合/Skill 串接
「我做完之後，別人會...」
```

Same knowledge. Different words. That's the entire gap.

同樣的知識。不同的詞。那就是全部的差距。

## Why This Matters for AI ── 這為什麼對 AI 重要

This isn't just about making people feel good. It's an **architectural necessity**.

這不只是讓人感覺良好。這是**架構上的必要性**。

In the [Five-Layer Model](../01-foundations/five-layer-model.md), Layer 3 (the Skill layer) is where domain expertise lives. The LLM provides raw intelligence (Layer 1), tools provide actions (Layer 2), but **Skills encode how work should actually be done** in a specific domain.

在[五層模型](../01-foundations/five-layer-model.md)中，第三層（技能層）是領域專長所在之處。LLM 提供原始智能（第一層），工具提供行動（第二層），但 **Skills 編碼了特定領域中工作應該如何實際執行**。

Who knows how work should be done? **The people who do the work.** Not the engineers. Not the AI researchers. The accountants, the project managers, the administrators, the educators, the marketers.

誰知道工作應該怎麼做？**做這份工作的人。**不是工程師。不是 AI 研究者。是會計、專案經理、行政人員、教育工作者、行銷人員。

If only engineers build Skills, we get technically elegant workflows that miss the domain nuances. If domain experts build Skills, we get workflows that actually work in the real world — with all the edge cases, shortcuts, and institutional knowledge baked in.

如果只有工程師建構 Skills，我們得到的是技術上優雅但遺漏領域細微之處的工作流程。如果領域專家建構 Skills，我們得到的是真實世界中實際有效的工作流程——包含所有的邊界情況、捷徑和組織知識。

**The Agentic future needs both engineers and domain experts building Skills.** Engineers for the infrastructure; domain experts for the knowledge. This is why making Skills accessible to non-technical professionals isn't a nice-to-have — it's the critical path to making AI actually useful.

**代理型的未來需要工程師和領域專家一起建構 Skills。**工程師負責基礎設施；領域專家負責知識。這就是為什麼讓非技術專業人士能建構 Skills 不是「有也不錯」——而是讓 AI 真正有用的關鍵路徑。

## Try It Yourself ── 自己試試看

We've built an interactive exercise called **[Skill Self-Discovery](../../skills/skill-self-discovery/)** that guides you through exactly this process.

我們建立了一個叫做 **[技能自我發現](../../skills/skill-self-discovery/)** 的互動練習，引導你走過這個過程。

It asks you seven simple questions about your everyday work — no technical knowledge required. At the end, it shows you that you just defined a complete Skill using the same framework used by production AI systems.

它問你七個關於日常工作的簡單問題——不需要技術知識。最後，它讓你看到你剛剛用與生產環境 AI 系統相同的框架定義了一個完整的 Skill。

If you're using Claude Code or Cowork, you can install this Skill and run it right now. If you're just reading the documentation, the [SKILL.md file](../../skills/skill-self-discovery/SKILL.md) contains a complete example session you can read through.

如果你在使用 Claude Code 或 Cowork，你可以安裝這個 Skill 並立即執行。如果你只是在閱讀文件，[SKILL.md 檔案](../../skills/skill-self-discovery/SKILL.md)包含一個完整的範例工作階段供你閱讀。

The experience takes about 10 minutes. Many people find it genuinely surprising — not because they learn something new about AI, but because they realize **they already knew it all along**.

這個體驗大約需要 10 分鐘。很多人發覺這真的很驚喜——不是因為他們學到了關於 AI 的新東西，而是因為他們意識到**他們一直都知道**。

## What Comes After Discovery ── 發現之後

Once you've experienced the self-discovery exercise, several paths open up:

一旦你經歷了自我發現練習，幾條路徑就打開了：

**Path 1: Polish your Skill** 路徑一：打磨你的 Skill. The rough Skill you defined can be refined — adding more specific validation rules, documenting edge cases you forgot, thinking about what Skills yours could connect with. The [Skill Builder](../../skills/skill-builder/) can guide this process.

你定義的粗略 Skill 可以被精煉——添加更具體的驗證規則、記錄你忘記的邊界情況、思考你的 Skill 可以與什麼 Skills 連接。

**Path 2: Build more Skills** 路徑二：建更多 Skills. Once you see the pattern, you start seeing Skills everywhere in your work. That weekly report you compile? Skill. The way you onboard new team members? Skill. How you review contracts? Skill. Each one you define makes AI more useful to you.

一旦你看到模式，你開始在工作中到處看到 Skills。你每週編寫的報告？Skill。你引導新團隊成員的方式？Skill。你審查合約的方式？Skill。

**Path 3: Teach others** 路徑三：教別人. Run the self-discovery exercise with your team. You'll be surprised how quickly people go from "I can't do AI" to "I just built my first Skill."

與你的團隊一起執行自我發現練習。你會驚訝人們從「我不會 AI」到「我剛建了第一個 Skill」的速度。

**Path 4: Go deeper** 路徑四：更深入. Read the rest of the [Agentic Substrate documentation](../../README.md) — the [Skill Anatomy](../01-foundations/skill-anatomy.md), the [Five-Layer Model](../01-foundations/five-layer-model.md), the [Design Patterns](../02-architecture/agentic-design-patterns.md). With the self-discovery experience as your foundation, these documents will make immediate, practical sense.

閱讀 [Agentic Substrate 文件](../../README.md)的其餘部分。有了自我發現的經驗作為基礎，這些文件會立即有實際的意義。

## A Note to Engineers Reading This ── 給正在讀這篇的工程師

You might be thinking: "This is too basic. My colleagues should just learn the framework."

你可能在想：「這太基礎了。我的同事應該直接學框架就好。」

Consider this: the most valuable Skills in any organization encode **domain expertise that no engineer possesses**. The HR manager who knows the subtleties of performance review processes. The legal professional who knows which contract clauses actually matter. The project manager who knows the real dependencies (not the ones in the Gantt chart).

想想這個：任何組織中最有價值的 Skills 編碼的是**沒有任何工程師擁有的領域專長**。知道績效考核流程細微之處的人資主管。知道哪些合約條款真正重要的法務專業人士。知道真正的依賴關係（不是甘特圖上那些）的專案經理。

If you want to build AI systems that actually work in the real world, you need these people contributing Skills. And the fastest way to get them contributing is to show them they already can.

如果你想建構在真實世界中實際有效的 AI 系統，你需要這些人貢獻 Skills。而讓他們開始貢獻的最快方式，是讓他們看到他們已經可以了。
